GOAL 2

ZERO HUNGER

AIM

Zero Hunger focuses on ending hunger and ensuring all people have sufficient, safe, and nutritious food year-round. It encourages sustainable agricultural practices, supports small-scale farmers, and aims to eliminate all forms of malnutrition, including stunted growth in children. The goal also addresses food waste and promotes food security.

       

TARGETS
  • 2.1: Universal Access to Safe and Nutritious Food.
  • 2.2: End All Forms of Malnutrition.
  • 2.3: Double the Productivity and Incomes of Small-Scale Food Producers.
  • 2.4: Sustainable Food Production and Resilient Agricultural Practices.
  • 2.5: Maintain the Genetic Diversity in Food Production.
  • 2.6: Invest in Rural Infrastructure, Agricultural Research, Technology and Gene Banks.
  • 2.7: Prevent Agricultural Trade Restrictions, Market Distortions and Export Subsidies.
  • 2.8: Ensure Stable Food Commodity Markets and Timely Access to Information.

CHALLENGES
  • Climate Change
  • Conflict
  • Economic Instability

SOLUTIONS
  • Sustainable agriculture practices: Promote agroecology and support smallholder farmers.
  • Food distribution networks: Enhance infrastructure to reduce food waste and improve access.
